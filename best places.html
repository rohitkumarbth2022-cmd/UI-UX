HISTORICAL PLACES IN PUNJAB

GOLDEN TEMPLE

The Golden Temple is a gurdwara located in Amritsar, Punjab, India. It is the pre-eminent spiritual site of Sikhism. It is one of the holiest sites in Sikhism, alongside the Gurdwara Darbar Sahib Kartarpur in Kartarpur, and Gurdwara Janam Asthan in Nankana Sahib.

WAGAH BORDER

The Wagah-Attari Border is the only international border crossing between India and Pakistan that is open for overland travel. It's famous for the daily "Beating Retreat" ceremony, a vibrant display of military precision and patriotism, attracting thousands of visitors daily. The border is located approximately 30 kilometers from Amritsar, India.

JALLIANWALA BAGH

Jallianwala Bagh is a historic garden and memorial of national importance close to the Golden Temple complex in Amritsar, Punjab, India, preserved in the memory of those wounded and killed in the Jallianwala Bagh Massacre that took place on the site on the festival of Baisakhi Day, 13 April 1919. The 7-acre (28,000 m2) site houses a museum, gallery and several memorial structures. It is managed by the Jallianwala Bagh National Memorial Trust, and will be renovated between 2025 and 2027.
